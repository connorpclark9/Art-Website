Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about discovering art and working with Kindred Seal.

Kindred Seal is a curated art broker, not a traditional gallery. Rather than maintaining a physical exhibition space, we focus entirely on connecting buyers with exceptional local artists through storytelling. Every artist on our platform has been hand-selected, and every piece comes with the context and narrative that makes it meaningful. We act as a bridge between you and the artist, handling the discovery and connection process so you can find work that truly resonates.

Our curation process is intentionally rigorous. We review every application personally, evaluating artists on the quality and consistency of their work, the originality of their voice, and the depth of their story. We look for artists who demonstrate genuine commitment to their practice and whose work reflects both technical skill and authentic self-expression. Not every applicant is accepted, and that selectivity is what ensures the quality of our roster.

The process is simple and personal. Browse our artist profiles and their available work. When you find something that speaks to you, reach out to us by emailing connorpclark9@gmail.com. We will answer any questions you have about the piece, provide additional images or details if needed, and arrange the purchase directly. There is no shopping cart or impersonal checkout — every transaction is handled with care.

We do not currently operate a physical gallery space, but we provide high-resolution images and detailed descriptions for every piece so you can get a thorough sense of the work before committing. If an in-person viewing is important to you, reach out and we will do our best to arrange something on a case-by-case basis, depending on the artist and location.

Prices vary depending on the artist, medium, size, and complexity of the work. In general, you can expect to find pieces ranging from a few hundred dollars to the mid-thousands. Each artwork listing includes pricing information, and we are always happy to discuss options that fit your budget. Our goal is to make collecting original local art accessible without undervaluing the artists who create it.

Shipping is handled on a per-purchase basis to ensure each piece is packed and delivered with the care it deserves. We work with professional art shipping services when needed and coordinate directly with you on timing and logistics. Shipping costs vary depending on the size of the work and your location, and we will provide a clear estimate before you finalize your purchase.

Because we act as a broker between you and the artist, returns and exchanges are discussed on a case-by-case basis. Your satisfaction is our priority, and we want you to feel confident in every purchase. If something is not right, reach out to us and we will work with you and the artist to find a fair resolution.

Yes, some of our artists are open to commissions. If you have a specific vision or project in mind, get in touch with us and we will connect you with an artist whose style and process align with what you are looking for. Commission availability, timelines, and pricing vary by artist, and we will guide you through the process from start to finish.

We would love to hear from you. Send an email to connorpclark9@gmail.com with a link to your portfolio or website, a brief artist statement, and a few samples of your best work. We review every application personally and will get back to you with our thoughts. There is no application fee or formal form — just reach out and introduce yourself.

We look for quality, originality, a compelling story, and a genuine commitment to your practice. Technical skill matters, but so does voice — we want to represent artists who have something meaningful to say through their work. We value consistency in your body of work, authenticity in your process, and a willingness to collaborate on telling your story to a wider audience.

We handle the parts of selling art that most artists find challenging or time-consuming. This includes professional storytelling and copywriting for your profile, photography coordination, building your dedicated artist page, connecting you with interested buyers, and ongoing marketing through our journal and social channels. Our goal is to free you up to focus on creating while we take care of getting your work seen.

Our commission structure is discussed during the onboarding process and is designed to be competitive and transparent. We believe in fair compensation for both the artist and the platform, and we are upfront about how the numbers work from the very beginning. There are no hidden fees or surprise deductions.

Pricing is a collaborative discussion between you and Kindred Seal. We take into account your experience, the medium and scale of the work, market context, and your own goals. We will never pressure you to underprice your work, and we will offer honest guidance to help you set prices that reflect the true value of what you create.

Absolutely. Kindred Seal provides non-exclusive representation. You are free to sell your work through your own channels, other platforms, or in-person at shows and markets. We see ourselves as an additional avenue for your work to reach new audiences, not a replacement for the relationships and outlets you have already built.

There is no penalty or pressure if a piece does not sell right away. Art finds its audience on its own timeline, and we understand that. We continue to actively market your work, feature it in relevant journal content, and connect it with potential buyers. Our commitment to our artists is ongoing — we do not drop anyone because sales are slow.
